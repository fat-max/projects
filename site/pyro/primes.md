Primes are **intermediate pyrotechnic layers** applied to reactive or decorative components to ensure **reliable ignition and fire transfer**. Their primary function is to bridge the gap between an ignition source and a composition that may be difficult to ignite directly, providing consistent and predictable initiation under controlled conditions.

#### Conceptual Function

A prime typically:

- Is **more ignition-sensitive** than the main composition it serves
- Produces sufficient heat, flame, or sparks to initiate that composition
- Burns in a controlled manner rather than producing a visual effect

Primes are commonly used on elements such as stars, pellets, delays, or other subcomponents where uniform ignition timing is critical to overall performance.

#### System Role

From a system perspective, primes contribute to:

- **Ignition reliability**, especially in low-oxygen or high-velocity environments
- **Timing consistency**, ensuring effects occur as designed
- **Operational safety**, reducing the likelihood of misfires or partial ignition

#### Safety and Regulation

Because primes involve energetic materials and directly influence ignition behavior, their design, application, and use are **closely regulated** and subject to strict manufacturing and handling standards. In professional pyrotechnics, primes are treated as **functional engineering interfaces**, not as effect-producing compositions.
